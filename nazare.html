A Day in Nazaré

A medieval pilgrimage town, a living fishing village, and the home of the biggest waves ever surfed — all in one clifftop-and-beach day.

Full day Beach + old town Family friendly

Nazaré makes the most sense as three towns layered on one cliff. Spend the morning by the beach, ride the historic funicular up to the old town, and let the giant-wave lookout be the finale.

A pilgrimage town

Sacred since the 12th century, built around the legend of Our Lady of Nazaré and a knight saved at the cliff's edge.

A fishing village

Fish still dry in the sun on the sand, and older women wear the traditional seven skirts.

Big-wave capital

An underwater canyon funnels Atlantic swells into record waves each winter. In July the sea is calm — the drama lives at the viewpoints.

1 The legend

The story dates to 1182: the knight Dom Fuas Roupinho, chasing a deer in thick fog, nearly rode off the cliff — he called out to Our Lady of Nazaré and his horse stopped dead at the brink while the deer plunged into the sea. A chapel, the Ermida da Memória, marks the spot; in 1377 King Fernando I founded the sanctuary that still crowns the cliff. The big pilgrimage falls on 8 September.

The seven skirts. The most-loved explanation: fishermen's wives counted the waves — seven for the sea to calm enough to land a boat — while waiting on the shore. Others say the seven days, the seven virtues, or the rainbow's colours.

2 How to spend the day

Morning · Praia

Beach & old fishing quarter

Start on the long, sheltered beach — calm and swimmable in summer. Wander the cobbled lanes, catch the drying-fish tradition (an open-air exhibit), and the market with fishermen's wives in traditional dress.

Late morning · The ride up

The historic funicular

Take the funicular to Sítio, the clifftop old town. It opened in 1889 and was designed by a disciple of Gustave Eiffel.

Midday · Sítio

Viewpoint, sanctuary & lunch

The Miradouro do Suberco for the postcard view, the sanctuary and legend-chapel, then grilled sardines at a Sítio tavern.

Afternoon · The waves

Fort, lighthouse & Praia do Norte

The fort and red lighthouse over Praia do Norte, where the record waves break, with a small exhibition. A steep 10-min trail drops to the beach and sea caves.

Late afternoon

Back to the sand

A last swim on Praia da Nazaré before the easy hour's drive home.

4 Up top: what to see in Sítio

The postcard

Miradouro do Suberco

A clifftop viewpoint ~110 m above the sea, steps from the funicular.

Faith & legend

Sanctuary of Nossa Senhora

The 14th-century pilgrimage church and the tiny Ermida da Memória by the cliff.

History & views

Fort of São Miguel Arcanjo

Built 1577 against pirates; the red lighthouse (1903) sits on top. Prime big-wave lookout.

For the teens

Praia do Norte & caves

Steep 10-min trail to the wild north beach and its sea caves. Look, don't swim — serious currents.

5 The famous waves

80–100 feet

The record-breaking winter waves at Praia do Norte. A deep underwater canyon channels and amplifies Atlantic swells here. In July the sea is flat and safe, so you enjoy the story from the fort.

7 Good to know

Getting there
~1 hour drive north from Atouguia da Baleia / Peniche.
Funicular
Links the beach (Praia) to the clifftop old town (Sítio).
Swimming
Praia da Nazaré is calm and family-friendly. Avoid Praia do Norte.
Bring
Sun protection, swimwear, comfy shoes for cobbles and the cliff trail, a light layer up top.
